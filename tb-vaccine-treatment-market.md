<p><p>The TB vaccine treatment market includes two main types of vaccines: immune vaccines and therapy vaccines. Immune vaccines aim to prevent tuberculosis (TB) infection by boosting the immune system's ability to fight the disease. These vaccines are usually administered to individuals who have not been infected with TB. Therapy vaccines, on the other hand, are designed for individuals who have already been infected with TB. They work by stimulating the immune system to target and destroy TB bacteria in the body, aiding in the treatment and control of the disease.</p></p>
