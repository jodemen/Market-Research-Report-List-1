<p><p>Clinical diagnostics automation refers to the integration of automated systems and instruments in the healthcare industry to streamline and improve laboratory processes. There are two main types: modular automation and total lab automation. Modular automation involves the use of different automated modules or instruments for specific tasks in the lab, allowing for flexibility and customization. Total lab automation, on the other hand, involves the complete automation of all lab processes, from sample handling to result analysis, providing higher efficiency and reduced human intervention. Both types aim to enhance accuracy, speed, and productivity in clinical diagnostics.</p></p>
