Gas chromatography involves separating the gas mixture into its individual components using a stationary phase and a mobile phase. Gas chromatography-mass spectrometry combines gas chromatography with mass spectrometry to identify the individual components based on their mass-to-charge ratio. Both techniques are essential for healthcare professionals to analyze and ensure the purity and quality of medical gases, such as anesthesia gases or respiratory therapy gases.</p></p>
