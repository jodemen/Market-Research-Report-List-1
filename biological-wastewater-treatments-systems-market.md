<p><p>Biological wastewater treatment systems use microorganisms to break down and remove organic pollutants from wastewater. There are two main types of these systems: aerobic and anaerobic. Aerobic systems require oxygen and utilize aerobic bacteria to degrade the pollutants, producing carbon dioxide and water as byproducts. In contrast, anaerobic systems do not require oxygen and use anaerobic bacteria to decompose the pollutants, producing methane and carbon dioxide as byproducts. Both types have their own advantages and applications depending on the wastewater characteristics and treatment goals.</p></p>
