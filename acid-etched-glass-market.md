<p><p>Acid-etched glass is a type of glass that has been treated with acid to create a frosted, translucent appearance. This process involves applying a special acid paste to the surface of the glass, which then etches away a thin layer of the glass, leaving behind a smooth, matte finish. Acid-etched glass is often used in architectural applications for privacy, light diffusion, and decorative purposes.</p><p>The acid-etched glass market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.8% during the forecast period. Acid-etched glass is commonly used in windows, doors, partitions, and other interior design elements in commercial and residential buildings. The market is also driven by the growing popularity of modern and minimalist design trends, which often incorporate acid-etched glass for its sleek and elegant appearance.</p><p>In addition, advancements in glass manufacturing technology have made acid-etching more cost-effective and environmentally friendly, further fueling market growth.
