<p><p>Seismic isolators are devices designed to protect buildings and structures from the damaging effects of earthquake-induced ground shaking. These devices act like shock absorbers, providing a buffer between the building and the ground, thereby minimizing the transmission of seismic waves to the structure. Seismic isolators are typically made from materials such as rubber, steel, and lead, which are capable of deflecting and absorbing the energy from seismic waves.</p><p>The global market for seismic isolators for buildings has been experiencing substantial growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a robust rate during the forecast period. The increasing awareness about the importance of earthquake-resistant construction and the stringent regulatory requirements for seismic safety of buildings are driving the demand for seismic isolators. Additionally, the growing investments in the construction of commercial and residential buildings, particularly in earthquake-prone regions, are further fueling market growth.</p><p>One of the key trends observed in the seismic isolator for buildings market is the adoption of advanced technologies and materials in the development of isolator systems.
