<p><p>Continuous Subcutaneous Insulin Infusion Devices are used by diabetic patients to deliver insulin continuously throughout the day. There are two main types of these devices - Normal Pump and Patch Pump. Normal Pump is a device with a tube connecting the pump to the cannula that delivers insulin, while Patch Pump is a tubeless device that adheres directly to the skin. Both types help diabetic patients maintain stable blood sugar levels by providing a consistent and controlled dose of insulin.</p></p>
