<p><p>Dual-energy computed tomography (CT) utilizes two different energy levels to differentiate materials with different atomic numbers, providing enhanced tissue characterization and lesion detection. Dual-source CT systems use two X-ray tubes and detectors positioned at different angles to acquire data simultaneously, improving image quality and reducing scan time. Rapid kVp switching CT systems allow for quick energy changes during acquisition, enabling more efficient dual-energy imaging. These advanced technologies expand the capabilities of CT imaging in various clinical applications.</p></p>
