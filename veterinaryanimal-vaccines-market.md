<p><p>The veterinary/animal vaccines market comprises various types of vaccines used to prevent and control diseases in animals. The types include live attenuated vaccines, which are made from weakened pathogens; inactivated vaccines, created from killed pathogens; toxoid vaccines, which use toxins produced by pathogens; recombinant vaccines, made by inserting genes into a live vector; and other vaccines, which include DNA vaccines and subunit vaccines. Each type of vaccine offers unique advantages and is used based on the specific characteristics of the targeted pathogen and the desired immune response in animals.</p></p>
