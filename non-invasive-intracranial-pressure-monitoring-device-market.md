<p><strong>The Non invasive Intracranial Pressure Monitoring Device Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Transcranial Doppler Ultrasonography</li><li>Tympanic Membrane Displacement (TMD)</li><li>Optic Nerve Sheath Diameter</li><li>MRI/CT</li><li>Fundoscopy (Papilledema)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Non-invasive intracranial pressure monitoring devices encompass a variety of methods including Transcranial Doppler Ultrasonography, which measures blood flow in the brain to indirectly estimate pressure. Tympanic Membrane Displacement (TMD) involves assessing changes in the eardrum in response to pressure changes. Optic Nerve Sheath Diameter is measured using ultrasound to gauge pressure in the brain. MRI/CT scans can detect abnormalities indicative of elevated pressure. Fundoscopy involves examining the back of the eye for signs of increased pressure, such as papilledema.</p></p>
