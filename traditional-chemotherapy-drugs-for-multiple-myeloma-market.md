<p><p>Traditional chemotherapy drugs for multiple myeloma consist of various types, including Melphalan, Vincristine, Cyclophosphamide, Etoposide, Doxorubicin, Liposome Doxorubicin, Bendamustine, and others. Melphalan inhibits cell growth by damaging DNA, while Vincristine interrupts cell division by inhibiting microtubule formation. Cyclophosphamide hinders DNA replication, Etoposide prevents DNA repair, and Doxorubicin and Liposome Doxorubicin inhibit DNA and RNA synthesis. Bendamustine combines DNA damage and cell cycle arrest mechanisms. Other drugs with distinct mechanisms may also be used to combat multiple myeloma. These drugs collectively aim to slow down or eliminate cancer cells in patients with multiple myeloma.</p></p>
