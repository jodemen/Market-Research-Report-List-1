<p><p>Sclerotherapy is a medical procedure used to treat varicose veins by injecting a solution into the affected veins, causing them to collapse and eventually fade away. Different types of solutions can be used in sclerotherapy, including detergents, osmotic agents, and chemical irritants. Detergents are substances that act like soap, breaking down the walls of the veins. Osmotic agents work by drawing water out of the vein walls, leading to their collapse. Chemical irritants, as the name suggests, cause irritation and inflammation in the veins, resulting in their closure.</p></p>
